Benefits of Gymnastics

The benefits of gymnastics are not simply physical. The continued practice and mastery of gymnastics also leads to several other benefits. Contributing to the production of well-rounded, healthy children and young adults.

What are the benefits:

Spatial awareness is also known as spatial recognition ability, and refers to the ability to grasp (recognize) things in three dimensions.

Three dimensions refers to space with such things as verticality, horizontal breadth, height, ups and downs, and depth, in other words how we normally feel spaces around us.

Through strengthing depth perception and improving coordination it keeps the brain sharp and plays a vital role in further development and enhancement.

Furthermore, as it also influences processes such as protocols, one can expect improvements in actual learning method themselves.

Thus, we would like to convey the fact that rather than solely making children study, the act of playing sports can lead to raising a more healthy and intelligent child.

About Us

The Balance Academy was created to share the passion and art of Rhythmic Gymnastics with the community.

At Balance Academy, we believe Rhythmic Gymnastics is much more than just exercise, and it should be treated as such. In addition to being a positive force in increasing self-confidence, gymnastics has also been associated with positive brain development in growing minds.

Through the practice of rhythmic gymnastics, students can increase their capacity for learning, and their ability to retain information. The balance and coordination used in the practice of rhythmic gymnastics have been known to improve a student’s learning comprehension, leading to long term memory improvement.

As students progress in the sport, they are learning, growing, and of course, having fun along the way. Balance Academy provides a variety of activities, programs, and competitions suitable for all genders, ages, abilities.

Sara,

Instructor

After her entry into elementary school, she started taking local rhythmic gymnastics classes with her parents’ hope for her developing and exceling into the sport. That was the beginning of her journey in rhythmic gymnastics. When she turned 15, she was selected to be a member of the national team and maintained her championship status throughout high school.

When she entered Tokyo Women’s College of Physical Education, a top college which has produced many Olympic athletes, she again continued her achievement as a top athlete throughout her college years.

Through many injuries in the hard and long trainings, she learnt various care methods and strengthening trainings. She then became qualified as a conditioning trainer to support young top athletes by providing them with proper knowledge of strengthening training after injury.

She believes that conditioning training enables retired athletes to lead healthy lives.
